MTTA · Brand Guidelines02 · The idea

The network is a person.

MTTA — the Metropolitan Toeesh Transit Authority — is a portfolio drawn as a transit map. Every line is a thread of toeesh's life; every station is something he made or can't stop thinking about. The brand borrows the discipline of real transit information systems: it is functional first, quiet, and every mark on the page earns its place.

The visual language runs in two registers of the same grammar. Paper — the printed rail map: a white field, solid ink station dots, white boards read via warm hairlines. And Signage — the night register: the black field of a station information screen, flat boards, cream advisories. Between them only the surface flips; every rule in this book holds on both.

  • Function first. Every element mimics a real piece of transit furniture — a departures board, a line map, a service advisory. If it wouldn't exist in a station, it doesn't exist here.
  • The field is black, the content is colour. Chrome is monochrome (black, white, grey). Colour belongs to the lines, and to the two sanctioned accents: signature yellow and signal red.
  • Flat, not decorated. No drop shadows, no gradients, no glows. Boards separate from the field by a header strip and a hairline, nothing else.
  • Lowercase voice. The system speaks quietly and in lowercase — except where signage convention demands otherwise (station names, advisories).

MTTA · Brand Guidelines03 · The roundel & wordmark

The origin aperture.

The mark is a roundel with an open centre — the origin aperture. It is the first station of the network (“the damn origin”) and the favicon, map marker and brand mark are all the same drawing. The hole is always a true cutout of the field behind it, never a printed second colour.

Construction

Outer circle Ø = 1u. Aperture Ø = 0.3u, dead centre. Clearspace = 0.4u on all sides. Minimum size 12px / 4mm.

Wordmark

Set in RM Neue at weight 800, tracking −0.05em: toeesh.network — the suffix always drops to secondary grey. Roundel sits left of the wordmark at 0.8× cap height.

on the field — white roundel on paper — ink roundel
  • Don't fill the aperture, rotate the mark, or add a ring/outline.
  • Don't recolour the roundel — it is white on the field, ink on paper. Line colours never touch it.
  • Do let the aperture show whatever surface it sits on: it is a hole, not a dot.

Nine colours. No more.

  • The field is true black — the map canvas and page background are the same surface, so boards float on it without edges.
  • Board / header / hairline are the only three greys of the furniture. A board is body colour, capped by a header strip, divided by hairlines.
  • White is for primary content and the highlight state — a selected row inverts to a white pill with black text.
  • Cream means advisory: service notices, onboarding, decommission stamps. Always black ink on cream, led by the !-roundel.
  • Signal red means closed / no service. Used in tags and closed-station markers only — never decoratively.
  • Yellow is the single signature accent: live readouts, the DUE row, the primary call-to-action.

  • RM Neue (variable, 100–900) is the only display and body face. Headlines run at weight 800, tracking −0.04em, tight leading (0.9–0.96). Helvetica Neue is the fallback stack.
  • JetBrains Mono appears in exactly one place: the departures board — the one true machine surface. Every other small label, tag, code and status is RM Neue at weight 600, uppercase, tracked +0.1em.
  • Numbers are tabular in readouts. Arrival times sit right-aligned, bold white, with the unit ("min") staying small.
  • No italics, no underlines in signage. Emphasis is weight or the white-pill inversion.

  • Boards: body colour, one header strip, hairline rows. Never a drop shadow, never a thick border.
  • Stations are solid dots sitting on the ribbon — ink on paper, white at night. Transfers are the white-disc bullseye (⊚). Names set as small bold text beside them — riding the segment's angle on diagonals — with letter-in-square feature icons (M media · ↗ links · ★ start here).
  • The land: a gray beltway ring frames the network; parks are pale-green commons; water is flat pale blue. Termini carry two-letter route codes (CE, MU, PR…) in their line's disc.
  • Selection inverts: white pill, black text. One highlight at a time.

MTTA · Brand Guidelines08 · Voice & motion

Quiet, lowercase, in service.

Voice

  • Lowercase by default. "the network", "slowly living", "start here ↘". Caps are reserved for station names and mono readouts.
  • Transit grammar. Everything is said the way a system would say it: "GOOD SERVICE · slowly living", "DELAYED · that side project", "NOW ARRIVING · a new idea".
  • Warm underneath. The deadpan signage voice carries a personal note — advisories apologise, quips joke, the map says "welcome".

Motion

  • Motion is ambience, not decoration. Trains ride the lines, the clock blinks, boards flip like split-flaps. Nothing bounces for attention.
  • Every animation has an off switch. The MOTION toggle freezes the whole system; the design must read perfectly when frozen.
  • The night-owl mode (Konami code) is the one sanctioned break from all of this — neon, glowing, loud. It is an easter egg, not a theme.
