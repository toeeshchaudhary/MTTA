I wanted one place. Not a feed I don't own, not a profile that looks like
everyone else's profile — one corner of the internet that's mine, where I can
put the things I make, the things I like, and the things I can't stop thinking
about at 2am.

So I built it like a subway map.

## why a map

Good transit design is honest. It tells you exactly where you can go and how to
get there, with nothing in the way. Massimo Vignelli's 1972 diagram threw out
geography and kept only what you need: lines, stations, color. Helvetica and a
grid. It's the most confident piece of information design I know.

That's the feeling I want this place to have. Pick a line. Ride it. Get off
where you want.

## the lines

- **The 1** is writing — this, and whatever I rant about next.
- **The 7** is projects — things I've built or am building.
- **The N** is likes and now — what I'm into, and what's got my attention
  this week.

Everything interchanges at home. The station never closes.

> No animations. No tracking. No infinite scroll. Just stops.
