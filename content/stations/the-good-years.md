the long, easy stretch. few stops, express service, no delays. the kind of run where you stop checking the departure board because the next train is always yours anyway.

we had it down to the minute. i knew your stops by heart and you knew mine, and the map felt bigger with you on it — like the whole network had grown one more colour that only the two of us could see.

you think a line like this just keeps running. you start to believe good service is a promise and not a season. i never once pictured the sign flipping to *not in service* — nobody does, not in the good years. that's what makes them the good years. you spend them certain.
