the end of the line. the last train pulled out one evening in '23 and simply never came back — no final announcement, no clean closure, just a schedule that quietly stopped being true.

here's the thing nobody tells you: they don't tear an abandoned line up right away. the rails stay. the platforms stay. you can walk the whole route in your head, stop by stop, and feel exactly where the current used to run. that's the cruel part — it still looks like it could reopen any morning. it can't.

so it stays on the map, greyed and dashed, out past where the living lines go. not because i think a train is coming. because some routes you keep drawing even after the service ends — you leave them on the diagram so you don't forget the network was ever this shape. ✕ last ran '23.
