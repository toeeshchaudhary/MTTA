i recently watched before sunrise, it's a romantic drama of olden times , and it made me remember this phrase i wrote when i was younger (i'm still young, but younger). 

it's a story about two people , an american who just had a break-up from a long distance relationship and a french girl, and they both have different characteristics about themselves.

the girl was a sweet and charismatic, yet closed person , while the american was a "go-getter" of sorts and was always thinking of random things, from historical events to each other's fears of death and various ideologies that we usually skip past when trying to make new friendships or relationships

it made me remember this phrase i wrote when i was head over heels for a person that didn't see me in the way i saw them. I have this tendency to create fantasies for people on the basis of they look and how they talked (even though i had never ever talked to them) and, i wrote this entire scene , anyways i'm getting sidetracked here.

i remember vividly sitting on the roof alone that evening and watching the sunset and clearly thinking "god, what a dreary place", because without the sunset, the roof i'm at would just be an ordinary abandoned roof, but with even the slight tender pinkness that you see in the skies during a sunset, it became a small oasis for me , a place where i could sit down , and recollect myself and truly realize how disconnected i've become.

school and your surroundings teach you to be a "social" person , which is how we survive, after all , we have feelings because we are social in our nature, we need to talk to people, not for work but for enjoyment and fulfillment and to have a sense of purpose. 

but in the midst of all this we forget to talk to ourselves, to sit down and just stare at the wall for an obscenely long period of time while thinking "hey that paint's chipping off and oh i feel like i'm scared of xyz thing",

that evening i felt weird, because it had been so long since i've thought about what i've wanted, and it made me question every single professional or personal decision i had made in my life up till that point of time. 

that day i sat down for a total of 2 hours , watching the sky go from daylight to pink to a darkness that felt comforting and warm and it consequently made me think over every single decision that i'd have made at that point of time and reflect on it , in a good way.

how does this relate to before sunrise? because the inter-person relationship that is depicted there is the ideal form of friendship and courtship, a relationship that is on the entire basis of trying to find out more about each other , without having any substantial influencing factors like money, embedded deep into it. 

the human mind desires connection , connection with thyself or connection with others, romantic or platonic does not matter, but just the simple "connection" does, that evening and this piece of media reminded me that, i had lost my connection with myself , and that i was slowly becoming sad , because i was always doing what others told me to do, "participate in xyz competition because your skills permit that" or "eww that's cringe , why do you even like that". small things that had built up over a gradual period of 2 years.

i had "become astray" from myself, and i've regretted every single moment that i didn't listen to myself, but put my needs below others, just belittling myself.

i'd strongly recommend you watch before sunrise, and the entire before trilogy, it's a must watch, something fresh from the same old stale hollywood movies of this year, and maybe , you'll have a similar epiphany like i had.
