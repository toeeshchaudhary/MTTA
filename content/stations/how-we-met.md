where the old flame line began. a chance transfer — the kind you don't plan, the kind that only happens because two routes cross for a single second and you step off at the wrong stop on purpose.

i wasn't looking to add a line to the map that year. the network felt finished. but you were standing on the platform like you'd always been part of the system, and something in me quietly rerouted the whole thing.

everything downstream traces back to here. every stop after this, every good year, every delay i pretended not to notice — all of it boarded at this platform. i still know exactly where it was. i think a part of me is still standing on it, waiting for a train that already ran.
